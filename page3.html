Our Products

Watercress

Watercress is a nutrient-rich leafy green with a peppery flavor often used in cooking to add a refreshing touch to various dishes. It is enjoyed in salads, sandwiches, and wraps for its crunchy texture and vibrant taste.

Watercress can also be paired with soups and stir-fries, where its pungent flavor stands out among other ingredients. In addition to its versatility, watercress is packed with vitamins and minerals, making it a healthy addition to any meal.

Whether eaten raw or cooked, watercress brings a unique and refreshing element to culinary creations. (Packaging – 50 grams and 200 grams)

Strawberries

Our juicy and sweet strawberries are perfect for pies, tarts, cakes, and other desserts. They also make a delicious topping for pancakes, waffles, and yogurt bowls.

They shine in salads, pairing well with savory ingredients like spinach and goat cheese. Beyond desserts and salads, strawberries can be blended into smoothies, sauces, and jams for a burst of summery delight in every bite.

Whether enjoyed fresh, cooked, or blended, strawberries are a delicious addition to any recipe. (Packaging – 200 grams)

Herbs

Basil:
A versatile herb with a sweet and peppery flavor, basil is widely used in various cuisines. Popular uses and cooking suggestions include pesto, salads, pizzas, soups, marinades, seasonings, drinks, garnishes, Italian cuisine, Thai cuisine, Mediterranean cuisine, and Vietnamese cuisine. (Packaging – 10 grams)

Chives:
Chives are frequently used as a garnish for soups, salads, and baked potatoes. They are mixed with softened butter to create herb butter. Chives add flavor to egg dishes like omelets, scrambled eggs, and quiches, and blend well with cream cheese sauces. They are perfect for sauces like hollandaise and béarnaise, and enhance potato, pasta, and green salads, as well as salad dressings. Culinary suggestions include using chives in French vichyssoise, Chinese stir-fries and dumplings, American baked potatoes and deviled eggs, Italian herb focaccia and risotto, Mexican guacamole and salsa, and German frikadellen breadcrumbs and herb butter. (Packaging – 10 grams)

Dill:
Dill is often used in pickling, fish dishes, and creamy sauces. It's great in potato salads, egg salads, and coleslaw, adding a fresh, tangy flavor. Dill works well in soups and stews, especially those that are seafood-based. It's also a popular herb for seasoning cured salmon. When cooking, try incorporating dill in Greek tzatziki, Scandinavian dill potatoes, Russian borscht, or a simple dill lemon butter fish. Its delicate fronds make a charming garnish for many dishes, enhancing both flavor and appearance. (Packaging – 10 grams)

Mint:
Mint brings a versatile touch to both sweet and savory dishes. Add fresh mint to classic cocktails like mojitos or mint juleps, while enhancing the flavor of fruit-based desserts like sorbets and fruit salads. In savory dishes, explore its Middle Eastern flair in tabbouleh salad and lamb kebabs, or pair it with peas in elegant soups and risottos. Consider sprinkling chopped mint over rich chocolate desserts or infusing it into cream for a delightful mint-flavored topping that adds a luxurious touch. Mint, with its vibrant flavor and aromatic essence, elevates any culinary creation. (Packaging – 10 grams)

Oregano:
A staple in Italian cuisine, oregano shines in tomato sauces, pasta dishes, and pizzas. Oregano also pairs well with Mediterranean flavors, enhancing grilled meats, roasted vegetables, and Greek salads. Its robust flavor makes it a key ingredient in marinades, soups, and stews, adding a savory depth to dishes. Additionally, oregano can be sprinkled on homemade bread or infused in oil for added flavor. Whether fresh or dried, oregano brings a touch of warmth and complexity to any culinary creation. (Packaging – 10 grams)

Parsley:
An essential component of Mediterranean tabbouleh salad, club sandwiches, and French herb bouquets, its vibrant green color and mild flavor elevate homemade pesto and chimichurri sauces. Sprinkle parsley over roasted vegetables, grilled fish, or pasta for a finishing touch or incorporate it as a main ingredient, bringing a hint of freshness. (Packaging – 10 grams)

Sage:
Sage adds depth to butter sauces for pasta dishes like ravioli or gnocchi. It also pairs well with roasted vegetables, especially when combined with garlic and olive oil. Crispy fried sage leaves make a delicious garnish for soups or salads. Whether fresh or dried, sage will add a unique flavor to any recipe. (Packaging – 10 grams)

Rosemary:
An aromatic herb that enhances dishes with its depth and fragrance. Often used in Mediterranean cuisine to flavor roasted meats, poultry, and potatoes, and to enhance bread and focaccia dough. Its woody stems are perfect for grilling skewers, adding an earthy note. Rosemary's versatility extends to marinades, sauces, and soups, its pine-like flavor complementing rich ingredients like garlic and olive oil. Whether fresh or dried, rosemary brings warmth and complexity to any dish. (Packaging – 10 grams)

Thyme:
It pairs well with poultry, lamb, and vegetables, adding depth to roasts or grills. In French cuisine, it's a key ingredient in the bouquet garni and herbes de Provence blends, enriching stews, soups, and sauces. Thyme also complements Mediterranean flavors like roasted tomatoes, grilled fish, and olive oil marinades. Whether fresh or dried, thyme brings a fragrant and savory note to any recipe. (Packaging – 10 grams)
